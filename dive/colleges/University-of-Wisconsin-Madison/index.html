OVERVIEW

Founded in 1848, The University of Wisconsin, Madison has been a catalyst for the extraordinary. As a public land-grant university and prolific research institution, its students and faculty members partake in a world-class education and solve real-world problems. One of the longest and deepest traditions surrounding the University of Wisconsin, the Wisconsin Idea signifies a general principle: that education should influence people’s lives beyond the boundaries of the classroom. Synonymous with Wisconsin for more than a century, this “Idea” has become the guiding philosophy of university outreach efforts in Wisconsin and throughout the world.
